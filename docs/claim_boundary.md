# Claim boundary

## Claimed

The package claims a conditional theorem stack for declared certified RCP/RCLM seed-library classes:

- accepted updates preserve protected distinctions under explicit non-loss and recovery gates;
- conservative-extension primitive words compose with additive loss/recovery budgets;
- direct-engine construction applies on declared certified engine domains;
- seed-domain entry and witness-library closure apply for declared certified seed libraries;
- successor-verification packet construction and finite/infinite trajectory closure apply for declared SV seed-library domains;
- controlled reference artifacts demonstrate finite non-vacuity for the canonical class;
- Lean 4 checks the canonical finite RCP/RCLM witness and refinement core;
- M3-Min defines a sufficient learned-entry certificate boundary.

## Not claimed

The package does not claim:

- arbitrary-system recursive self-improvement;
- broad capable learned-agent entry;
- universal successor trust;
- full L"obian/Vingean reflection;
- automatic semantic completeness of the declared goal object;
- automatic reality containment;
- unbounded practical tractability;
- empirical validation of frontier-scale systems;
- full mechanization of the complete math and architecture papers.

## Lean certificate boundary

The Lean 4 certificate covers the canonical finite RCP/RCLM witness, finite checker core, and RCLM-to-RCP refinement module. It does not mechanize the entire theorem stack.

## Artifact boundary

The controlled artifacts are executable replay artifacts for declared finite reference classes. They are not evidence that arbitrary trained models enter the theorem domain.
